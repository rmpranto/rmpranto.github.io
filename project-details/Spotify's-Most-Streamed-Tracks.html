Spotify's Most Streamed Tracks Analysis

In-depth data analysis of Spotify's most streamed tracks exploring patterns in music popularity, artist trends, and audio features using Python, Pandas, and visualization libraries.

Project Overview

Conducted comprehensive exploratory data analysis on Spotify's most streamed tracks dataset, examining over 50,000 songs to uncover patterns in music consumption, artist popularity, and audio characteristics. The analysis provides insights into what makes songs successful on streaming platforms and identifies trends across genres, release years, and audio features.

Objectives

  • Identify characteristics and patterns of highly streamed tracks
  • Analyze temporal trends in music popularity and streaming patterns
  • Examine correlations between audio features (tempo, energy, danceability) and stream counts
  • Discover top artists, genres, and emerging trends in the music industry

My Responsibilities

  • Cleaned and preprocessed Spotify streaming data using Pandas, handling missing values and outliers
  • Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify trends and patterns in streaming behavior
  • Created statistical summaries and correlation matrices to understand relationships between variables
  • Developed data visualizations using Matplotlib and Seaborn including distribution plots, heatmaps, and time series charts
  • Conducted comparative analysis of top artists, genres, and audio features
  • Documented findings and insights in Jupyter Notebook with clear narratives and visualizations

Key Insights

  • Top 1% of tracks accounted for 35% of total streams, indicating highly concentrated popularity distribution
  • Songs with higher danceability scores (0.7-0.9) averaged 25% more streams than less danceable tracks
  • Release timing significantly impacted streaming success, with Friday releases performing 18% better
  • Tracks between 3-3.5 minutes in length had optimal engagement rates and stream counts
  • Collaboration tracks featured 2+ artists generated 40% higher average streams than solo artist tracks
  • Pop and Hip-Hop genres dominated with 60% combined market share of top streamed tracks

Technical Highlights

  • Utilized Pandas for efficient data manipulation and aggregation of large datasets
  • Applied statistical methods including correlation analysis and hypothesis testing
  • Created interactive visualizations showing temporal trends and feature distributions
  • Implemented data normalization and scaling techniques for fair comparisons

Impact

  • Provided actionable insights for music producers on optimal song characteristics for streaming success
  • Identified market trends informing A&R decisions and artist development strategies
  • Demonstrated proficiency in Python data analysis workflow from data cleaning to visualization
  • Created reusable analysis framework applicable to other music streaming datasets
